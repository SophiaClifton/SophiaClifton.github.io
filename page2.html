CS Games


The CS Games competition is the largest computer science competition in Quebec each year. Every mid-March, it brings together between twenty and thirty teams of ten undergraduate students from around ten universities in North America. Teams must participate in demanding competitions covering all areas of computing: from web and mobile development to artificial intelligence, cybersecurity and more practical areas such as robotics.

Particularly, I participated in the Information of Teconology challenged, which involved reverse engineering Raspberry software and searching for hidden flags.
Additionally, I participated in the Art Forgery Challenge which involved replicating given images using HTML and CSS in limited time.

EngWeek 2024 XTREME Programming Competition

2nd Place Winner


I participated in an 6 hour programming competition for Concordia's Engineering Week.

I solved the The "BracketOps Challenge" which required participants to develop a BracketOps Resolver, capable of evaluating strings representing a complex mathematical expression using the four types of brackets to indicate operations, along with integers and floating-point numbers.The outputted result being the evaluated expression as a float, rounded to four decimal places.

To solve this challenge and meet its constraints, I employed stack structures to keep track of the various operations and current values.

The coded solution and challenge description are available on GitHub.

Skills:

- Data structures and algorithms
- Java
